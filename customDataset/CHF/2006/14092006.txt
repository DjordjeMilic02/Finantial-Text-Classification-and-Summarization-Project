National Bank raises the target range for the three-month Libor by 0.25 percentage points to 1.25%–2.25%
The Swiss National Bank (SNB) is raising the target range for the three-month Libor with immediate effect by 0.25 percentage points to 1.25%–2.25%. It intends to hold the rate in the middle of the target range for the time being.
The economy continues to develop favourably. The recovery is broadly based and this is having a positive effect on the labour market. The SNB is raising its forecast for GDP growth in 2006 to just under 3% and expects average inflation for the year to attain 1.3%.
By raising the target range, the National Bank is further adjusting its monetary policy stance to developments in the economy. With this move, the SNB is ensuring that the inflation outlook remains favourable.
